Tips and tricks


These are some of the tips and tricks that you can find out about hijab


Daily hijab style tips and tricks to level up your fashion game
  1. Skip the Pinning Step

    2. Pick a long rectangular scarf, wrap it around your head and throw one end of the scarf to one side of
    the shoulder. Make sure that the scarf is long enough to create the desired look and would not fall
    apart with time.

  2. Vibrant Hued Scarf

    3. Make your casual look bright and appealing with some intricately printed scarfs. This fashion trick goes
    perfectly with the modern day settings and casual events. The colour of the outfit and the scarf should
    be in perfect sync with each other so that you can give your best shot in conventional dressing while
    looking equally stylish too.

  3. Accessorising is a Key

    4. One of the best hijab fashion tips is to use a piece of attractive head jewellery and style it accurately.
    Be a piece of necklace, headband, a pair of earrings or a pretty handbag; a tiny add-on can give an
    edge to your modest appearance and make it awe-inspiring.

  4. Style with Glasses

    5. While wearing cute glasses, make sure that you wrap your scarf loosely around the head and tuck it
    neatly to ensure the best fashionable look. Funky frames are the next big thing in the world of fashion
    and are what ruling all over the hearts of the modern fashionistas. 

How to care for your hijab
  1. Don’t rub while washing

    2. Don’t brush or squeeze your hijab too hard. It is recommended to only soak your hijab in the detergent
    until the dirt is clean. Gently rub only if necessary.

  2. Avoid washing using washing machine

    3. Using washing machine might be harsh for hijab fabric. It is advice to hand wash your hijab to make
    sure you use the right pressure while cleaning it.

  3. Soak in softener within 15 minutes

    4. Soak your hijab in mix solution of detergent and softener around 15 minutes and then rinse it. Softener
    help making hijab feel softer and smell nice.

  4. Wash hijab separately from other clothes

    5. This is to prevent the colour of your hijab from being affected by the other clothes if the colour stain.

  5. Avoid hanging it under direct sunlight for too long

    6. Hang your hijab under shaded roof as direct sunlight will faded out the colour of hijab. Bring in your
    hijab as soon as it already dry after hanged.

  6. Iron with appropriate temperature

    7. Make sure you know the right temperature for each type of hijab fabric. Choosing the wrong
    temperature might cause burnt or damaged. Usually, only use low temperature while ironing.

  7. Practice proper storage

    8. Store hijab in cool and dry place avoiding direct sunlight. There are a lot of storage method such as
    hanging, folding, in a box or drawer.

Hair care for hijabi
  1. Dry your hair first

    2. You shouldn’t tie your hair when it’s wet as wet hair is prone to breaking. Always allow your hair to
    dry naturally. Use a blow-dryer sparingly.

  2. Don’t tie your hair tightly

    3. It’s best to tie your hair a little looser so as not to strain the roots of the hair from constantly being
    pulled back.

  3. Let your hair down

    4. When at home let your hair loose. Open up your hair, take out the hair tie, and run your fingers
    through the hair and gently massage scalp to get some circulation going.

  4. Give you hair a weekly treat

    5. Use hair mask, hair conditioner, hair serums, and oils. Give some treatment to your hair to let it rest
    from being under hijab most of the time.

  5. Don’t wash your hair everyday

    6. Scarves protect hair from the damaging UV rays as well as various environmental pollutants.
    Therefore, we don’t need to wash/shampoo our hair on a daily basis. However, do wash your hair if it
    became oily.

  6. Wash under-caps regularly

    7. Our hair releases oil and dead cells which sticks to the under-cap. Make it a habit to regularly wash
    your under-caps since these are in direct contact with the hair for several hours each day.

  7. Wear the right type of scarf

    8. It is preferred to wear scarf of breathable fabrics such as cotton, chiffon or silk. Not only are these
    fabrics light, they also will not make your head feel heavy or suffocated.

Getting starts on wearing hijab
  1. Wear it for the sake of Allah (SWT)

    2. Make the intention to wear the hijab for the sake of your creator. Always remember that Allah is your
    main supporter for the changes.

  2. Make the decision to start

    3. Set a day and don’t back down from your decision. Ask Allah for strength, and remember the power
    of patience and faith.

  3. Ignore the outside noise

    4. Remember, you won’t stop being yourself because of the outside noise. When you start wearing the
    hijab, you might come across a lot of judgment but remind yourself of your intention and why you are
    doing it.

  4. Take it one day at a time

    5. Take baby steps when starting out. Try wearing the hijab when going out every other day and soon
    with the help of Allah (SWT) and patience, one day will turn into weeks, then months. Before you know
    it, your hijab will become second nature.

  5. Work your way up to proper hijab

    6. You might choose to start wearing modest clothing first, such as long blouses or long coats, with long
    skirts until you are comfortable with wearing a headscarf as well. Once you are comfortable wearing
    modest clothing and a headscarf, you might be ready to wear the attire towards your proper hijab.

  6. Give yourself a chance and have faith in Allah (SWT)

    7. No matter what anyone says, making the decision to start wearing hijab is between you and your
    Creator. You may stumble, you may even fall, but try and try again and you will reach your goal.

  7. Start with a friend

    8. It’s only natural for us to seek moral support, especially when embarking on a new journey. Starting
    this journey with a friend of family member will not only provide you with the support and motivation
    you need, but will increase you in rewards.

Organizing your hijab

Make sure your hijab are always organize and kept in a proper ways.

  1. Classify hijab into group

    1. Fabric
      2. Don't mix different type of fabrics as you might want to use a certain type of fabric
      according to your comfort on different occasion.

    2. Colour
      3. It will make it easier to see the collection of colours you have to match with your clothes.

    3. Occasion
      4. Arrange your hijabs occasion wise like casual, semi-formal, formal, and fancy.

    4. Type
      5. Separate type of scarves such as square scarve, shawl and turban so you can easily decide
      which style you want to go for the day.

    5. Size
      6. It is hard to recognize the size of hijab in fold or roll form. So, arranged according to the size
      help you to identify the hijab sizes or cutting.

  2. Storage options

    1. Hanger
      2. Hanging make hijab easier to look for and helps to reduce wrinkles.

    2. Fold
      3. Folding will make your hijab arranged neatly.

    3. Roll
      4. Rolling your hijab help to save space in your storage.

    4. Drawer
      5. You can either roll or fold your hijab and kept in a drawer to keep it away from sunlight.

    5. Box
      6. Store hijab into classified box for each group.
